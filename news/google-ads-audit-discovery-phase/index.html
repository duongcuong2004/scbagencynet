Google Ads audit discovery phase: deep digging, key steps to maximize advertising effectiveness!
Original Fat Cat Sheriff Starry Sky Out to Sea
November 25, 2024 13:30
The discovery phase in the Google Ads audit is the first critical step. This step lays a solid foundation for optimizing the campaign and maximizing the return on advertising expenditure (ROAS).
At this stage, we advertisers have to look at the account data systematically, figure out what works, what doesnt work, and where there is room for improvement.
This process requires analyzing the account structure, targeting settings, keyword performance, ad quality, bidding strategy, and conversion tracking, so that a roadmap for better advertising can be created.
Whether you are managing advertising for a small business or a global brand, the discovery phase of the audit can help you find insights that can drive change and influence. Come, lets talk about the key steps and components of a successful Google Ads discovery phase.

  1. Analyze Account Structure
    A well-organized account structure is the foundation of an effective Google Ads campaign. During the discovery phase, looking at the layout and segmentation of campaigns and ad groups can help you identify areas where you may not be as efficient.
    Campaign segmentation: Are campaigns logically grouped by theme, product category, or goal? If so, relevance will be improved and management will be simpler.
    Ad group organization: Each ad group should focus on a specific, closely related set of keywords, so that the ads are more relevant to user searches.
    Impact on Quality Score: A well-organized structure will have a positive impact on Quality Score, which may reduce costs and improve ad ranking.
    Evaluating account structure in this way is very important to improve relevance, quality score, and overall ease of account management.
  2. Targeting and audience analysis
    You have to understand that knowing who can see your ads is very important to your Google Ads strategy. Targeting options can directly determine the success or failure of your campaign. During the discovery phase, it is very important to look at the parameters of audience targeting.
    Demographic and geographic settings: You need to make sure your ads reach people of the right age, gender, location, and household income level that match your business goals.
    Device and time settings: If certain devices or time periods are more effective, then your targeting settings should reflect this.
    Audience lists: Looking at audience lists (like remarketing or custom intent audiences) is a great opportunity to maximize engagement with relevant users and prospects.
    Effective targeting is key to connecting with the right audience and reducing wasted ad spend.
  3. Keyword strategy and search query analysis
    Keywords are the core of Google Ads, and a careful review of your keyword strategy will reveal a lot of areas for improvement.
    Keyword match types: Look at how broad match, phrase match, and exact match types are used to find opportunities to optimize targeting and reduce irrelevant clicks.
    Negative keywords: A strong negative keyword list can prevent your ads from appearing on irrelevant searches, so you don’t waste ad spend.
    Search Query Reports: By looking at actual search queries, you can better understand what users are thinking and adjust keywords to improve ad relevance and click-through rate (CTR).
    By analyzing these elements, you can optimize your keyword strategy to better match user intent and minimize the money spent on poorly performing terms.
  4. Evaluate ad copy and quality score
    The quality and relevance of ad copy have a huge impact on ad performance. During the discovery phase, evaluate ad copy from these aspects:
    Ad relevance: You need to make sure that the ad copy directly targets the keywords in each ad group. Higher relevance will increase quality scores, reduce costs, and improve ad ranking.
    Engagement metrics: Analyze CTR and other engagement metrics to see which ads are performing best.
    Use ad extensions: Extensions like sitelinks, callouts, and structured snippets make ads more visible and more attractive to clicks.
    Evaluating ad copy quality can identify areas that need improvement and create more attractive and effective ads.
  5. Bidding strategy and budget allocation
    The way you bid is critical to ensuring your ads are showing at the right time and not overspending. During the discovery phase, check these things:
    Current bidding strategy: Are you using the best strategy, such as manual CPC, target CPA, or maximize conversions? The right strategy depends on your specific goals.
    Budget allocation: Look for opportunities to move budget from campaigns that are not performing well and give it to campaigns that are returning higher returns.
    Adjust bids by device or location: If certain devices or locations are performing better, you may need to increase bids there to get more valuable traffic.
    Examining these areas will help you spend more wisely and maximize the performance of every dollar of advertising.
  6. Conversion tracking and analysis
    Without accurate conversion tracking, you cant measure the true effectiveness of your campaigns. A key part of the discovery phase is to ensure that all conversions are tracked correctly:
    Conversion actions: Make sure every valuable action is tracked, such as purchasing something, submitting a form, or signing up.
    Pixel and Code Location: Verify that the tracking code is installed correctly and is capturing the right data.
    Google Analytics Integration: By integrating with Google Analytics, you can get a more complete picture of user behavior on your site.
    Setting up and reviewing conversion tracking gives you a clear picture of your ROI, which is always helpful in making future decisions about how to spend your advertising dollars.
    Benefits of a Comprehensive Discovery Phase in Google Ads
    There are many benefits to investing time in a discovery audit:
    Improved targeting: If you take the time to audit in the discovery phase, you will know how you are performing. With this knowledge, you can optimize your targeting better so that you can reach the most relevant audience.
    Higher Quality Score: If your account structure is well organized and your keywords and ad copy are highly relevant, your quality score will go up.
    Data-driven optimization: If you have a clear picture of what is working and what is not working, you can make strategic changes. Over time, the results will get better and the costs will go down.
    In general, if this discovery phase is executed well, it will lay a solid foundation for the continued optimization and growth of Google Ads.
    Conclusion
    The discovery phase in the Google Ads audit is not a preliminary audit that is just a formality, it is a critical in-depth study. From analyzing the account structure to looking at ad copy, positioning, keywords and conversions, each element here plays its role in developing a comprehensive strategy.
    If you invest energy in this stage, your Google Ads campaign will not be far from success, you can get better results and maximize your ROI.
