Facebook advertising account classification: bill account, overseas account, corporate account, balance account and personal account introduction

Facebook, Insights 8/20/2024 pm2:58
  1. SCB AgencyHome
  2. Insights
  3. Facebook

Bill Account

A billing account generally refers to an account used to record and manage the flow of funds, especially information records of fund activities (such as transactions, payments, refunds, etc.) between customers and commercial organizations. These fund activities may include information such as debts, accounts receivable and payable, and reconciliation. The billing account is centered on fund activities and records the flow process and status of money in detail.

NO.1 Features

  1. Fund flow records: The billing account mainly records the inflow and outflow of funds, including key information such as transaction amount, transaction time, and transaction type.
  2. Reconciliation and settlement: Through the billing account, commercial organizations can easily reconcile with customers to ensure the accuracy and transparency of fund flows. At the same time, the billing account is also an important basis for settlement.
  3. Management functions: Billing accounts usually have certain management functions, such as querying transaction records, generating financial statements, setting account permissions, etc., which help commercial organizations better manage their fund flows.

NO. 2 Application scenarios

  1. Cross-border e-commerce: In cross-border e-commerce, billing accounts are used to record cross-border payments, refunds, logistics costs and other capital flows, helping merchants and consumers understand capital changes during transactions.
  2. Internet advertising: In the field of advertising, billing accounts may be used to record advertisers’ advertising costs, click costs, conversion costs, etc., which helps advertisers evaluate advertising effects and manage budgets.
  3. Other business activities: In addition to cross-border e-commerce and advertising, billing accounts are also widely used in other business activities that require recording and managing capital flows, such as financial services, supply chain management, etc.

Enterprise Account

The term corporate account generally refers to the type of account that a company registers on a specific platform or system and uses for commercial activities.

Specifically, a corporate account is an account opened by a company on a relevant platform (such as an e-commerce platform, social media platform, advertising platform, etc.) for the purpose of cross-border trade, brand promotion, customer interaction or advertising.

Compared with personal accounts, corporate accounts usually have more business functions and tools, which can meet the diverse needs of companies in cross-border business.

NO.1 Features

  1. Rich business functions: Enterprise accounts usually provide rich business functions, such as product management, order processing, payment settlement, marketing promotion, data analysis, etc., to help enterprises operate cross-border business efficiently.
  2. Brand image display: Enterprise accounts allow enterprises to customize account information, upload corporate logos and promotional materials, and display the brand image and strength of the enterprise.
  3. Convenient customer interaction: Through enterprise accounts, enterprises can establish direct contact with customers, provide customer service, answer customer questions, collect customer feedback, etc., to enhance customer stickiness and loyalty.
  4. Accurate advertising: Enterprise accounts opened on the advertising platform can be used to place advertisements based on the characteristics and interests of the target audience, achieve precise marketing, and improve advertising effectiveness.

NO. 2 Application scenarios

  1. Cross-border e-commerce: On cross-border e-commerce platforms, business accounts are used to display and sell goods, process orders and payment settlements, and communicate and interact with customers.
  2. Social media marketing: On social media platforms, business accounts are used to publish brand dynamics, promote products and activities, interact and communicate with fans, and enhance brand awareness and influence.
  3. Advertising: On advertising platforms, business accounts are used to formulate advertising strategies, place advertisements, monitor advertising effects, and optimize and adjust based on data.

Individual Account

NO.1 Features

  1. Personal identity authentication: Personal accounts usually require users to provide real identity information for authentication to ensure the security and compliance of the account. For example, when creating a personal account on Facebook, users need to provide personal information such as name and email address, and perform real-name authentication.
  2. Wide range of uses: Personal accounts are widely used in the cross-border industry. They can be used in a variety of scenarios such as social media communication, e-commerce platform shopping, and payment platform transfers.
  3. Restrictions and risks: Compared with personal accounts, corporate accounts or commercial accounts may enjoy more permissions and benefits in cross-border business, but personal accounts also have certain restrictions and risks.

NO. 2 Application scenarios

  1. Social media communication: Individual accounts are very common on social media platforms. Users can keep in touch with friends, family, colleagues, etc. through individual accounts, and share life and work experiences.
  2. E-commerce platform shopping: On cross-border e-commerce platforms, individual accounts allow users to browse and purchase goods from all over the world.
  3. Payment platform transfer: Individual accounts also play an important role on payment platforms. Users can use individual accounts to perform cross-border transfers, collections, and other operations to meet the cross-border funding needs of individuals or families.

Overseas Accounts

Overseas accounts usually refer to accounts opened overseas, also known as overseas bank accounts or offshore accounts.

These accounts are opened by individuals or companies in non-domestic banks overseas to handle cross-border capital flows, investments, asset management and other business activities.

These accounts usually have higher capital freedom and flexibility, and can meet the needs of cross-border business transactions.

NO.1 Features

  1. High degree of capital freedom: Overseas accounts allow funds to flow freely abroad, without being restricted by domestic foreign exchange controls, facilitating cross-border investment, trade and asset management.
  2. Multi-currency accounts: Overseas accounts usually support multiple currencies, making it convenient for companies to conduct transactions and settlements in different countries and regions, reducing exchange rate risks.
  3. Privacy protection: Compared with domestic accounts, overseas accounts may be more stringent and secure in terms of privacy protection, which helps protect the financial information of individuals and companies.
  4. Investment convenience: Through overseas accounts, individuals and companies can more easily participate in overseas stocks, bonds, and other financial products investment, and achieve diversified asset allocation.

NO. 2 Application scenarios

  1. Cross-border trade: For enterprises with cross-border business dealings, overseas accounts are important tools for handling international payments, letters of credit and other businesses.
  2. Overseas investment: Individuals and enterprises can use overseas accounts to invest in overseas stocks, real estate, insurance and other investments to achieve a global layout of assets.
  3. Immigration and study abroad: For people who have the need to immigrate or study abroad, overseas accounts are convenient for paying tuition fees, living expenses and other expenses abroad, reducing the inconvenience and cost of cross-border remittances.
  4. Tax planning: In some cases, overseas accounts can also be used for tax planning, reducing the tax burden of enterprises or individuals by making reasonable use of the tax policies of different countries.

Balance Account

Balance accounts, also known as discount accounts, refer to the amount of money that has been pre-charged in the advertising account, which is usually sold to advertisers at a discount.

After the advertiser purchases a balance account, he can use the funds in it to place advertisements without having to pay additional advertising fees.

NO.1 Features

  1. Pre-charge: The funds in the balance account are pre-charged, and advertisers can choose balance accounts of different denominations to purchase according to their needs.
  2. Discounts: Balance accounts are usually sold at a discount price, and advertisers can purchase the same amount of advertising fees at a price lower than the original price, thereby saving advertising costs.
  3. Convenient to use: After purchasing a balance account, advertisers can directly use the funds in it for advertising, without the need for cumbersome recharge and payment processes.
  4. Risks and restrictions: Because it is a personal account, the balance account may have a low weight and be easily blocked, and usually has a daily consumption limit (such as a daily consumption limit of 50). In addition, some balance accounts may not provide after-sales service, and once the account is blocked, the balance in it may not be recovered.

NO. 2 Application scenarios

Balance accounts are widely used in cross-border advertising, especially for advertisers who want to save advertising costs and improve advertising efficiency.

By purchasing balance accounts, advertisers can get the same amount of advertising fees at a lower cost, so as to carry out cross-border advertising more effectively.
